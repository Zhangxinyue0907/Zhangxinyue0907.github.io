21 May 2020 - ARE YOU HUNGRY ?

Chinese ivory carving has a very long history, which began in the Neolithic age. As early as the Paleolithic age, people living in Zhoukoudian were buried with ivory carvings as decorations. "Historical records · Weizi aristocratic family" has recorded: "Zhou started as Xiang Zan." The emperors of Liao, Jin, yuan, Ming and Qing dynasties used Ivory as their royal offerings. There were workshops for making ivory products for imperial palaces in orchard factories in Ming Dynasty and manufacturing offices in Qing Dynasty. The most representative one is the famous ivory work "yueman Qingyou". Continue reading...
